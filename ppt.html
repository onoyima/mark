What is the Policy Cycle?

A framework describing the stages in creating and implementing public policy.


It's a cyclical process where policies can be reviewed, adapted, or abolished after implementation.

Key Actors in the Process

Official Government Bodies

Executives, Legislators, Judges, Bureaucrats, Special Commissions.

Non-Governmental Participants

Interest Groups, Citizens, NGOs, The Media.

The Process: From Problem to Proposal

1. Problem Identification

Recognizing a public need or dissatisfaction (e.g., fuel scarcity, poor education) that requires government action.

2. Agenda Setting

Influential stakeholders (media, political parties) transform problems into manageable issues for the government's official agenda.

3. Policy Formulation

Developing solutions by researching options, analyzing costs/benefits, and crafting specific policy proposals with stakeholder input.

The Process: From Law to Lesson

4. Policy Adoption / Legitimization

Formally approving the policy, giving it legal authority through legislative passage and executive signing.

5. Policy Implementation

Putting the policy into action by allocating resources and creating infrastructure. This is often the most challenging stage.

6. Policy Review

Assessing the policy's effectiveness, identifying consequences, and using the results to modify, overhaul, or terminate the policy.
